1 Peter

The First Epistle of Peter addresses Christians living as strangers in the world, calling them to holy conduct, patient suffering, and hope in the resurrection. Studied verse by verse with patristic commentary.

Wisdom of Solomon

A deuterocanonical book of the Old Testament, the Wisdom of Solomon meditates on divine wisdom, the nature of the righteous soul, and God's guidance of Israel. Cherished by the Church Fathers and read at many Orthodox services.

Galatians

St. Paul's letter to the Galatians defends the Gospel of grace against those who would add legal requirements to faith, and sets out the fruit of the Holy Spirit as the mark of life in Christ. A foundational text for understanding Paul's theology.

2 John

The Second Epistle of John is one of the shortest books in the New Testament: thirteen verses addressed to the "elect lady" and her children. The group spent several sessions working through every verse, drawing on patristic commentary.

Genesis

The book of beginnings: creation, the fall, the flood, and the call of Abraham. The group approaches Genesis with the Church Fathers as guides, reading it alongside Orthodox patristic commentary on creation and human nature.

During Catechesis Season

Bible Study pauses each year when the Catechesis series begins, typically in January and running through Holy Week. During that period, Wednesday evenings shift to formal instruction for those entering the Church and anyone who wishes to join. Bible Study resumes after Pascha.
