About the Council

Elected to serve

The Parish Council assists the rector in the administration of the Cathedral

The Parish Council of Saints Peter and Paul Orthodox Cathedral is elected annually by the Parish Assembly to assist the rector in the administration of the Cathedral. Council members are not administrators in a corporate sense — they are parishioners who have taken on a particular responsibility of service to their community, accountable to the Assembly and to God.

The Council oversees the temporal affairs of the Cathedral: its finances, its property, its programs, and its relationship to the wider Diocese of New York and New Jersey. In all things it works in partnership with the rector, in fidelity to the Statutes of the Orthodox Church in America.

Receiving reports from the treasurer, approving the annual budget, and ensuring the Cathedral's resources are directed toward its mission.

Serving on the Council

The Council is chosen by the parish, from the parish

Council members are elected at the Annual Parish Assembly, held each year on a Sunday following the Divine Liturgy. Any parishioner in good standing is eligible to vote and to serve — having received Holy Communion at least once in the past year, belonging to the parish for at least one year, and regularly fulfilling their financial obligations to the community.

If you are interested in parish governance or wish to learn more about how the Council works, reach out to the parish office or speak with Father Solomon after any service.

Parish Assembly

Minutes of Annual Parish Assembly

January 26, 2025

A quorum was present and the meeting was called to order at 12:25 PM. Father Solomon opened the meeting with a prayer.

Minutes

The minutes of the 2024 Annual Parish Assembly were approved by the assembly. Motion: Andrei Anghel / Nick Rus.

Pastor’s Report

The 2024 church year included two weddings, five baptisms, and two chrismations. Average Sunday attendance was 70 parishioners, reflecting a 27% increase from the prior year of 55. Aiden Harwood joined Ero Ogbevire as an altar server; they lead six boys who also participate.

The choir participated in baptisms and other services. Bible study classes include 15 people on average, both in person and online. Coffee hour discussions include 10-12 people.

An Akathist for the Three Holy Hierarchs was held in January. Father reported on our participation at the Inter-Parish Associations (IPA) initial meeting, a collaborative effort supported by the Assembly of Bishops.

Renovation projects completed include: mold remediation for 107 Grand; church chandelier cleaned; choir loft restored with new chandelier, brass safety rail, and remediated water damage; additional pump installed in church hall for flood protection. Liturgical fans were acquired. Relics of our patron Saints Peter and Paul are now on display.

Archbishop Michael will be with us February 22 and 23.

Gleb Ivanov reported six musical events last year: three School of Music children’s recitals and three church concerts. The church received $3,700 through this fundraising initiative. The Pascha concert will be on May 2, 2025.

Financial Report

Church finances were presented by Andrei Anghel. Total income for the year (excluding major contributors) was $136,572 — a 7% increase over the prior year. All contributions totaled $288,685. However, excluding top contributors and stock donations, we showed a deficit of $68,977.

Our largest expense was church restoration projects, totaling $27,600. An anticipated expense of $60,000 to repair the sunken floor was noted.

Motion to accept the financial report and budget for 2025: Charles George / Raymon Geis. Approved by the Assembly.

The auditor’s report was presented by Father in the absence of the auditor, Jessia Anghel, who reviewed the church’s books through December 31, 2024, and found them in good standing. Motion to approve: Laura Johnson / Lorraine Morrow. Approved by the Assembly.
