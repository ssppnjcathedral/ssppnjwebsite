Why We Give

Christian stewardship is an act of worship

Not a transaction — a participation in the life of the Church

This parish has kept its doors open through two world wars, a century of change in Jersey City, the economic pressures of the 1970s, the transformation of the neighborhood around it, and a Cathedral designation none of its founders could have imagined. It has kept its doors open not through endowments or institutional wealth, but through the faithfulness of ordinary people who gave what they could, week after week, generation after generation.

Your gift — whatever its size — is part of that same tradition. It is what pays the utilities so the candles don't burn alone in a cold building. It is what keeps the choir singing. It is what allows a priest to be present when someone walks through the door for the first time and doesn't quite know why they came.

What Your Gift Supports

Gifts to Saints Peter & Paul Cathedral support the full life of the parish: the maintenance of our historic building at 111–113 Grand Street, the ministry of our clergy, the musical program, the outreach to our neighborhood, and the resources to welcome inquirers and form new members in the Faith.

Parishioners are encouraged to consider a tithe — ten percent of their income — as the historical standard of Orthodox Christian stewardship. Whatever you are able to give, it is received with gratitude and used faithfully.

Other Ways to Give

In addition to online giving through Tithe.ly, you may give by check made payable to "Saints Peter & Paul Orthodox Cathedral" and mailed to 109 Grand Street, Jersey City, NJ 07302. Cash may be given at the candle stand or in the weekly offering.

For questions about planned giving, memorial gifts, or bequests, please contact the parish office at (201) 434-1986 or [email protected].
