About Our Burial Grounds

Where the faithful
await the Resurrection

In the Orthodox Church, burial of the dead is an act of love and theology. We bury our own, in consecrated ground, with full rites — because we believe in the resurrection of the body and the life of the world to come. The grave is not the end; it is a resting place, a kind of sleep, until Christ calls all things to himself.

Saints Peter & Paul has been caring for its dead since the earliest decades of the parish. Today we are stewards of two burial sites in Jersey City: the active Graceland Memorial Park, where plots are available for parishioners and their families, and the Historic Jersey City & Harsimus Cemetery — a National Historic Register site that holds some of our oldest graves and serves as a monument to the immigrant communities who built this city.

For burial arrangements, plot inquiries, or memorial services, please contact the parish office. We walk alongside families through every step of this sacred process.

Orthodox Burial

The Rite of Burial

The Church provides full burial rites for all parishioners: the Parastas memorial liturgy, funeral service, graveside prayers, and ongoing commemoration at the Divine Liturgy. Contact the rector to discuss arrangements.

Cemetery I Active · Plots Available
Active Parish Cemetery

Graceland
Memorial Park

Acquired by the Parish · 1960

Graceland Memorial Park has served as the active burial ground of Saints Peter & Paul for over sixty years. In 1960, the parish secured this cemetery to ensure that Orthodox Christians in the Jersey City community would have a dignified, consecrated place to bury their faithful departed in accordance with the Church's rites.

Parish plots are available for registered parishioners and their immediate families. Arrangements for burial, plot purchase, and memorial services are coordinated through the parish office. The cemetery is maintained by the Cathedral.

Cemetery II National Historic Register
Historic Parish Cemetery

Historic Jersey City
& Harsimus Cemetery

National Register of Historic Places · Jersey City National Historic Site

The Historic Jersey City & Harsimus Cemetery is one of the most significant burial grounds in New Jersey — a remarkable nineteenth-century survivor that holds the graves of some of Jersey City's earliest immigrant communities, including many of the families who founded this very parish.

Recognized on the National Register of Historic Places, this cemetery is both a living memorial and a historic landmark. Saints Peter & Paul maintains its connection to this site as part of its stewardship of the community's history and the memory of those who built the Cathedral with their hands and their sacrifice.

Arrangements How to Request Burial
The Process

Requesting interment
at Graceland Memorial Park

We walk alongside families at every step
I

Contact the Parish Office

Reach out by phone or email as early as possible. Our office is open Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM–5:00 PM. We will help coordinate all aspects of the burial with you and your family.

II

Meet with the Rector

Rev. Solomon Longo will meet with the family to discuss the funeral rites, the Parastas memorial liturgy, and the graveside service. All parishioners in good standing are entitled to full Orthodox burial rites.

III

Plot Selection & Preparation

The parish administrator will assist with plot selection at Graceland Memorial Park. Available plots are allocated to registered parishioners and their immediate family members on a first-come basis.

IV

Burial & Ongoing Commemoration

Following burial, the departed are commemorated at the Divine Liturgy on Saturdays of the Souls and at Radonitsa (Tuesday after Bright Week). Families may request individual Parastas services at any time throughout the year.
