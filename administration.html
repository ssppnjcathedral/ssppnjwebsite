Diocesan Leadership

The Church Hierarchy

Saints Peter & Paul Cathedral is under the omophorion of:
Metropolitan
☨
His Beatitude Tikhon
Archbishop of Washington, Metropolitan of All America and Canada

His Beatitude Metropolitan Tikhon is the Primate of the Orthodox Church in America. He guides the life of the Church across the United States, Canada, Mexico, and beyond.

Learn More at OCA.org →
Archbishop
☨
His Eminence Michael
Archbishop of New York and the Diocese of New York & New Jersey

His Eminence Archbishop Michael oversees the Diocese of New York and New Jersey, of which Saints Peter & Paul is the Cathedral church.

Learn More at OCA.org →

Cathedral Clergy & Staff

Acting Rector
☨
Rev. Solomon Longo
Acting Rector, Saints Peter & Paul Orthodox Cathedral

Father Solomon serves as the pastor and teacher of the parish and presides over liturgical worship of the Church. He was appointed to the parish by Archbishop Michael in June 2023. Father Solomon is available for pastoral conversations, confessions, and inquirer meetings.

Learn More →
☨
Gleb Ivanov
Interim Music Director

Maestro Ivanov is a classical concert pianist who has served as music director of several Orthodox parishes over the last 15 years. He leads the Cathedral choir in the liturgical singing of the Divine Services.

Learn More →
☨
Marius Anghel
Parish Council President

Marius serves as President of the Parish Council, overseeing the temporal affairs of the Cathedral in cooperation with the rector and in service to the parish community.

Learn More →

Parish Council
Elected by the Parish Assembly

The Parish Council assists the rector in the administration of the Cathedral, overseeing its temporal affairs and helping to carry out the mission of the Church in Jersey City.

Parish Council

The Parish Council assists the Parish Priest in the administration of the Cathedral

The Parish Council oversees the temporal affairs of the Cathedral in cooperation with the rector, and is responsible for the spiritual and material needs of the Parish, the encouragement of others to participate in the life and mission of the Parish, the Diocese, and the Orthodox Church in America.
