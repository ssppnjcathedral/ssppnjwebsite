Welcome

A living community,
one hundred and fifteen
years on Grand Street.

Founded in 1907 by immigrants from the Austro-Hungarian and Russian Empires, Saints Peter & Paul has been a place of worship, refuge, and fellowship in Jersey City for over a century. Elevated to a Cathedral in 2020, we remain what we have always been: a parish — one that gathers every Sunday, feeds one another at the table, and keeps the ancient Faith alive in a changing city.

Whether you are exploring Orthodoxy for the first time, seeking a parish home in Jersey City, or returning to the Faith of your fathers — you are welcome here. Come as you are.

Parish Life

The Community in Worship & Fellowship

Saints Peter & Paul is not only a place of prayer. It is a community of people who have gathered around the same table for over a century, through feast and fast, joy and sorrow, immigration and homecoming.

After every Divine Liturgy on Sunday, the parish gathers for coffee hour. On great feast days, the tables overflow. At Pascha, we stay through the night together. In the baptismal font, new members are born into the family. At the graveside, we do not leave one another alone.

This is what it means to be a parish. Not a congregation that meets once a week, but a family that shares a life.

Parking

Parking is available on Sundays in the gated lot directly across from the church on Grand Street. Street parking is also available in the surrounding neighborhood.

What to Wear

Modest, respectful attire. Come as you are — you will be welcomed warmly regardless. Women traditionally cover their heads inside the church, though visitors are not expected to do so.

Stewardship

Sustaining the work of
one hundred and fifteen years

This parish has kept its doors open through two world wars, a century of change in Jersey City, and a Cathedral designation — not through endowments, but through the faithfulness of ordinary people. Your gift, large or small, keeps the lights on, the choir singing, and the doors unlocked for the next person who needs to find us.
