Who We Are Today

A Living Community

Saints Peter and Paul Orthodox Cathedral is a 120 year old community living a millennia old faith. We are Orthodox Christians helping one another to follow Christ in every way according to the faith delivered by the apostles, taught by the church fathers, and lived by every generation.

Walk in on a Sunday morning and you will hear it before you see it: the sound of children. There are close to thirty of them now, running through the narthex after Liturgy, sharing food at the coffee hour table, learning the hymns alongside their parents. This parish is full of young families, and that fact, more than anything else, tells you what kind of place it is.

The people who call this home come from everywhere. Families from Egypt, Ethiopia, Russia, Georgia, Ukraine, and Romania worship side by side with American converts who found the ancient Faith and never looked back. You will hear different accents in the narthex and see different backgrounds at the coffee hour table. What holds it together is not a shared ethnicity but a shared life in Christ, and the conviction that this life is meant to be lived together.

The parish has nearly tripled in size over the past five years, largely through a wave of Americans encountering Orthodoxy for the first time. People arrive as inquirers, spend months or years learning and attending, and are eventually baptized or chrismated here.

The Heart of It

A Place to Put Down Roots

This is not just a place you attend on Sundays. You baptize your children here. You watch your kids grow up alongside children they have known since birth. You grieve together when there is loss and celebrate together when there is joy. You eat together after every Liturgy. You clean up the park on a Saturday morning and come back that evening to pray Vespers.

The family is at the center of everything here, both your own household and the whole church family. Orthodox Christianity has always understood the Faith as something handed down: from parents to children, from one generation to the next, not through argument but through life shared together. The greatest expression of Christian love is the strengthening of the local community. Not only the people under your own roof, but the people you pray with, eat with, and bury.

We hope this is the kind of place where your children's children could be baptized. Not a stop along the way, but a home.

Music & Worship

The Choir

We are deeply blessed in our music. Gleb Ivanov, a world-renowned classical pianist, serves as our music director, and the choir he leads includes professional opera singers, a Juilliard-trained classical flutist, Byzantine chant specialists, and other gifted musicians and artists who give their talent to the worship of God every week.

We say this with quiet pride and genuine gratitude: Saints Peter and Paul has what is, we dare say, one of the finest Orthodox parish choirs in the diocese. It is not an achievement to boast about. It is a gift we did not earn, and we try to hold it that way. Week after week, these people show up and give their best. The congregation is blessed by it every time they walk through the door.

Looking Ahead

Growing Together

Something is happening here. The wave of Americans finding their way to Orthodoxy is not an accident, and it is not slowing down. People in their twenties and thirties, and sometimes much older, are encountering the ancient Faith and choosing to stay. They come curious and leave convinced. Many of them bring their families. The parish is growing not because we are marketing ourselves but because the Faith is true and people are finding that out.

Children and families after the Divine Liturgy
The parish has nearly tripled in size over five years, and the growth is not slowing down. Fr. Solomon Longo, Rector

The natural next step, for a community that believes the Faith is handed down through families and children, is to ask what that looks like in practice. We are exploring the possibility of a parish preschool and children's school. Nick Rus is leading the working team, consultants have visited, and Archbishop Michael has expressed his support for the vision. God willing, this becomes something real.

The next generation of the Faith

Further out, we hope to see an Orthodox classical education academy take shape: a school rooted in the Faith, formed by the tradition, and built for the children who are growing up here. These are hopes, not promises. But they are the natural fruit of a community that takes seriously what it means to raise children in the Church.

1907
Parish Founded
Saints Peter and Paul Parish was established by immigrants from the Austro-Hungarian and Russian Empires, on October 15, 1907.
1909
109 Grand Street
The parish purchased the former First Reformed Church of Jersey City at 111-113 Grand Street for $35,000, giving the community a permanent home.
2000
Restoration
A six-year restoration effort, supported by three New Jersey Historic Trust matching grants totaling $586,736, preserved the building for another century.
2020
Elevated to Cathedral
The parish was elevated to the Cathedral of the Diocese of New York and New Jersey.
2023
Fr. Solomon Appointed Rector
Father Solomon Longo was appointed rector in June 2023. Gleb Ivanov began serving as Director of Music.
